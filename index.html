Line Follower Robot

❮ ❯

I programmed a TI-RSLK to follow a black line by implementing a Proportional-Integral-Derivative (PID) controller which allowed the robot to dynamically adjust its trajectory to the desired path. The robot uses an array of photoresistors and IR LEDs at its underbody to determine its location relative to the black line. Kp and Kd values were determined experimentally. The robot was able to reliably complete the course in 13 seconds.

FSAE Fuel Tank

❮ ❯

This is a FSAE race car fuel tank that I helped design. After researching and conducting tests on several materials, we finalized the material to Aluminum 5052 for its weldability and chemical resistance. My design of the filler neck simplified the manufacturing process and reduced its weight by 20%.

Fuel Tank Baffles

❮ ❯

I was tasked to design baffles for the fuel tank to mitigate fuel sloshing and starvation. I used CFD to validate my designs and also performed real-world testing with a model tank and 3D-printed baffles. I used MATLAB and Python to analyze the data to determine which baffle design performed the best. The final selected design was the trapdoor baffle, which increase the damping ratio to 0.86

Stacked Acrylic Keyboard Case

❮ ❯

This is a mechanical keyboard case that I designed using stacked acrylic. Since the keyboard PCB was not a standard platform, cases for this PCB were very expensive. The case is composed of 7 sheets of acrylic cut with a laser cutter and assembled with screws around its border. I also designed and 3D printed keyboard feet stands for this case. By designing and building my own case, I saved $120.
